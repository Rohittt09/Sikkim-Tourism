Sikkim Pilgrimage Tourism

Rumtek Monastery

Rumtek Monastery, located about 24 km from Gangtok, is one of the largest and most famous monasteries in Sikkim. Also known as the Dharma Chakra Centre, it was originally built in the 16th century by the 9th Karmapa and later rebuilt in the 1960s by the 16th Karmapa after fleeing Tibet. This monastery serves as the main seat of the Karma Kagyu lineage of Tibetan Buddhism outside Tibet.

Built in traditional Tibetan style, Rumtek is an architectural marvel with a massive prayer hall, intricate murals, and a Golden Stupa housing the relics of the 16th Karmapa. The complex also includes monastic quarters, a college for monks, and a large courtyard used for traditional masked dances during festivals like Losar and Kagyu Monlam. Rumtek is not just a religious center but also a place for cultural learning, attracting scholars and tourists from around the world.

Richenpong Monastery

Richenpong Monastery is a small but culturally rich monastery located in the peaceful town of Richenpong, West Sikkim. Surrounded by lush greenery and panoramic views of the Himalayas, this monastery offers a tranquil retreat away from the busy tourist routes. Though not as large as some others, it holds great local significance and provides a deep insight into Sikkimese Buddhist traditions.

The monastery is often visited for its calm setting and scenic surroundings, especially during sunrise when the golden light bathes the hills and the monastery walls. It houses Buddhist scriptures, prayer wheels, and thangkas (Tibetan paintings), and is an ideal place to witness the daily life of young monks in training. During important festivals, the monastery becomes a lively hub of prayers, rituals, and local participation.

Pemayangtse Monastery

Pemayangtse Monastery, located near Pelling in West Sikkim, is one of the oldest and most important monasteries in the state. It was established in 1705 by Lama Lhatsun Chempo and belongs to the Nyingma sect of Tibetan Buddhism. The name “Pemayangtse” translates to “Perfect Sublime Lotus,” symbolizing spiritual purity. Set on a hilltop overlooking the Rabdentse ruins, the monastery offers spectacular views of Mt. Kanchenjunga and the surrounding valleys.

The three-storeyed structure is adorned with traditional Tibetan architecture, featuring intricate wood carvings and vibrant murals. One of its most remarkable features is the seven-tiered wooden structure called "Zangdok Palri," which represents Guru Rinpoche’s heavenly abode. The monastery also serves as a training center for young monks and hosts important festivals like the Cham Dance during Losar (Tibetan New Year). Pemayangtse is known for its spiritual depth and artistic brilliance, attracting both pilgrims and cultural tourists.

Ranka Monastery

Ranka Monastery, also known as Lingdum Monastery, is a visually stunning and relatively newer monastery situated around 20 km from Gangtok. Built in the late 20th century, it follows the Zurmang Kagyud lineage of Buddhism. Nestled in a forested hillside with a panoramic view of the surrounding mountains, the monastery is famous for its peaceful ambiance and scenic beauty.

The monastery complex features a large prayer hall adorned with golden statues, elaborate murals, and colorful thangkas. It serves as a learning center for many young monks and is open to visitors who want to observe or participate in daily prayer rituals. It’s especially lively during Tibetan New Year celebrations, when monks perform Cham dances and offer prayers for peace and prosperity.

Lachen Monastery

Lachen Monastery, also known as Ngodrub Choling, is a prominent Buddhist site located in the picturesque village of Lachen in North Sikkim. Established in 1858 by Lama Karchen Dorje Drak, the monastery follows the Nyingma tradition of Tibetan Buddhism. Set against a backdrop of snow-capped mountains and alpine forests, it offers a serene environment ideal for meditation and spiritual learning.

The monastery houses a statue of Guru Padmasambhava, along with sacred texts, traditional paintings, and colorful prayer flags fluttering in the mountain breeze. Lachen Monastery is also known for hosting annual rituals and festivals such as the Torma Festival, which attracts local devotees and tourists alike. The interior is adorned with intricate murals, wooden carvings, and traditional Buddhist symbols. It serves as a spiritual hub for the Lachenpas and is often visited as a part of the route to Gurudongmar Lake.
