About Sikkim

Sikkim is a beautiful state in northeastern India, nestled in the Himalayas. Known for its breathtaking landscapes, rich culture, and biodiversity, it is a paradise for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts. Here are some key highlights:

Geography

Sikkim is the second smallest state in India, bordered by Nepal, Tibet, Bhutan, and West Bengal. It is home to the majestic Kanchenjunga, the third highest peak in the world, and boasts lush valleys, pristine lakes, and dense forests.

Culture

Sikkim is a melting pot of cultures, with influences from Nepal, Tibet, and Bhutan. The state is known for its vibrant festivals like Losar, Saga Dawa, and Dasain. Buddhism and Hinduism are the predominant religions, and the state is dotted with ancient monasteries and temples.

Biodiversity

Sikkim is one of the most biodiverse regions in the world. It is home to rare species like the red panda, snow leopard, and Himalayan black bear. The state is also known for its rich flora, including over 5,000 species of flowering plants.
